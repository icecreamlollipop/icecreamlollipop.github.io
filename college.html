Here Are the College Courses I've Taken!

I'm currently a junior student at CU Boulder, and expect to graduate around May 2022. I'm currently pursuing not one, but two Bachelors of Science in the School of Engineering and Applied Science: one in Computer Science, and another in Applied Mathematics. Note that CU Boulder's Applied Mathematics program was ranked 14th in the nation by the US News & World Report in 2018 (Source), ranked among the top 10 by the National Research Council in research productivity (Source, Source) , and is considered one of the best Applied Math programs (Source).

Furthermore, I was accepted into CU's Bachelor's-Accelerated Master's Computer Science Program. This means that I can expect to graduate with a Master's in Computer Science in May 2023. Thus, I will only spend a total of 5 years to earn both a Bachelor of Science and a Master's in Computer Science.

Anyway, what exactly are the courses I've taken in college? I have them all listed out here, as well as links to my Github repos for each class:

Computer Science Courses

  • Intro to CS (CSCI 1300)
    Languages: C++
    My Github Repo
    What's This Class About? The absolute basics of C++. Think stuff like "Hello World!". It was alright, but not very interesting.

  • Data Structures (CSCI 2270)
    Languages: C++
    My Github Repo
    What's This Class About? Data structures! One of the things I'm told to expect on interviews. I absolutely loved this class (partly because my instructor, Ashutosh Trivedi, was an incredible teacher - he even brought us candy every Friday). I was very proud of my final project: a program that computes the longest common substring between two (fake) genomes in O(nlogn) time. Data structures used include suffix arrays, longest common prefix arrays, and hash tables. (By the way, this project was inspired by this playlist by William Fiset)
